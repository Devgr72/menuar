# Project Overview — MenuAR

## Vision
MenuAR is the operating system for restaurant menus. We start with one killer feature — AR dish visualization — and expand into the full restaurant customer experience layer.

## One-line pitch
"Point your phone at any restaurant menu and see every dish in 3D on your table — no app needed."

## The Problem (with specifics)
- India receives 17M+ foreign tourists annually — most struggle with Indian dish names
- 68% of diners admit to ordering "safe" options because they don't know what exotic dishes look like
- Restaurants lose 20-30% potential upsell revenue because customers don't understand premium dishes
- Current solution: Google it at the table (awkward, slow, leaves the restaurant's ecosystem)

## The Solution
A single QR code on each restaurant table. Scan → camera opens → point at physical menu → 3D dish models appear directly on the table. Tap any dish to see it in full detail with AI-generated descriptions, ingredient lists, and price context. Works in 20+ languages automatically.

## Competitive Advantage

### Why we win
1. **WebAR (no app)** — Zero friction. Competitors with native apps have 80%+ drop-off at install step.
2. **One QR, whole menu** — Competitors put QR on each dish. We put one on the table. Simpler for restaurants, better for customers.
3. **AI content generation** — Restaurants don't manually write 100 descriptions. We auto-generate. Our content quality compounds over time.
4. **3D model library** — Every model we create is a moat. Our library will have 10,000+ dishes before any competitor starts.
5. **Translation built-in** — Automatic based on phone language. No configuration. Critical for tourist areas.

### Current competitors
- **Kabaq** — AR menus, requires app download, US-focused, expensive
- **Augmented.city** — enterprise AR, not food-specific
- **Yelp/Zomato photos** — 2D only, not integrated into the menu experience
- **QR menu apps** (DotPe, Petpooja) — digital menus, no AR

**Our gap:** No WebAR menu solution exists in India. We're first.
